LevelUpLearning

LevelUpLearning was a project I did for The Iron Yard; The goal was to create an application or a website that solves a problem. I’ve always been interested in the use of technology in education, so I focused to make a solution for students in elementary to middle school who are struggling with school and give them an alternative method of teaching; learn through interaction instead of lecture.

You can view the app Live or on GitHub

Made using: Illustrator, Photoshop, HTML, CSS and Sass, jQuery, Jekyll

Process

Since I was designing for kids, I needed to make sure that the app’s design was clean and straightforward. I went through multiple iterations to find a structure that was easy to understand as well as responsive. In my mind the final product would be largely used on a tablet, but with the capability of students being able to work at home on their (or their parent’s) computer.

Like most projects, it was hard not to jump right into the design process immediately. I had a rough concept in mind when I thought up this project, but needed to do more research to justify my reasoning. I looked through apps and websites that already existed out there to get a sense of what looked good and what didn’t; the imagery they used, the different methods of teaching, I even looked at common school curriculums and what subjects students had the hardest time grappling with.

After researching came wireframing. I knew I wanted to focus on gamification to keep a child's attention. Positive reinforcement was key to making a child feel accomplished, and it was important that the student would be challenged but engaged. Thus I added the badge system; a student completes a lesson and earns a badge.

I needed a brand that was inviting yet educationally stimulating. I had originally come up with three different directions to take; energetic and fun, studious, or childlike and playful. I ended up mixing the three, focusing on friendliness and being inviting.
